Overview

Tic-Tac-Toe is a game with a 3x3 grid of empty squares. Two players, one playing as X and one playing as O, take turns placing their pieces on the board. Player X goes first, and pieces can only be placed in empty squares. As soon as one player has three pieces in a single row, a single column, or in either of the diagonals paths, they have won the game. If all squares are filled and no player has three in a row, the game is a tie.

Scenario

There is one scenario in this game: playing against another player.

Scenario: Player 1 plays against Player 2

The Game Play Screen

2 players open the game to play Tic-Tac-Toe. When the program starts, a 3x3 grid appears on the screen.A 3x3 grid appears on the screen. Players decide who goes first and second. The first player gets the symbol “X” and the second gets “O”. When the player 1 clicks on the one of the boxes an X appears there.

The Game


Player 2 gets the turn next. He clicks on the box on the grid and O appears there. Nothing happens if player 2 clicks on the same box as player 1.
The players then continue to take turns and click on the grid to put a symbol until one of the player gets their symbol in a row, a column or diagonally and that player wins. Player 1 gets X in a row and “X Wins” appears on the screen.

How to play again

The “Replay” button appears on the grid. Player 1 presses the button and the "Play" screen reappears. The players start to play the game. They play games until one wants to leave. The player closes and leaves the game.
